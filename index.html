How to Conduct an L10 Meeting

1. Segue (5 minutes):

Purpose: Set a positive and focused tone for the meeting.

Activities: Quick team introductions (optional, especially for larger teams). Good News Sharing: Each member shares a personal or professional win to build rapport and positivity.

2. Scorecard Review (5 minutes):

Purpose: Quickly assess the overall health of the company based on key metrics.

Activities: Review 3-5 pre-defined key metrics from your company scorecard. Briefly compare actual performance against targets. Identify any significant deviations and note areas requiring deeper discussion later.

3. Rocks Review (5 minutes):

Purpose: Evaluate progress on the team's top priorities, also known as "Rocks."

Activities: Briefly review each Rock (typically 3-7 quarterly priorities). Clearly state whether each Rock is "on track" or "off track." For off-track Rocks, discuss roadblocks and potential solutions.

4. Core Values (5 minutes):

Purpose: Recognize and celebrate instances where team members embodied the company's core values.

Activities: Share specific examples of how core values were upheld in recent situations. Discuss the positive impact of these actions. Recognize and appreciate team members involved in upholding the company's values.

5. To-Do List (10 minutes):

Purpose: Clearly define next steps and ensure accountability.

Activities: Review all action items identified during the IDS discussion. Assign ownership for each action item to a specific team member. Clearly define deadlines for each action item.

6. IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve) (60 minutes):

Purpose: This is the heart of the L10 meeting, dedicated to tackling the most pressing issues.

Activities: Identify Issues: Brainstorm and discuss current challenges or obstacles facing the company. Discuss Issues: Analyze each issue from different angles to understand its root cause. Solve Issues: Collaboratively develop solutions and action plans to address the problems. Prioritization: Rank issues based on their potential impact and urgency.

7. Conclusion (5 minutes):

Purpose: Summarize key takeaways and next steps.

Activities: Briefly recap the main decisions and action items from the meeting. Assign someone to take minutes and distribute them to the team. Optional: Collectively rate the meeting on a scale (e.g., 1-10) to identify areas for improvement.
